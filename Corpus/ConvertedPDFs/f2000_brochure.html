3M F2000 compomer restorative
is an easy-to-use system that
offers improved handling
characteristics that save you time:
• Does not stick to instruments
• Does not slump
• Adapts well to margins
• Easy to shape and contour
• Easy to polish
3M Dental research and
development has taken the
compomer concept – combining
the positive attributes of a glass
ionomer with those of a
composite – to a new level.
After years of experience with
both glass ionomer and composite
technology, producing this
compomer was a natural extension
of 3M Dental’s expertise.
The easy-to-use 3M™ F2000
Compomer Restorative is a
one-part, light-curable filling
material designed to be used
with 3M™ Single Bond Dental
Adhesive. 3M F2000 compomer
restorative offers dentists a
fluoride-releasing restorative with
superior handling characteristics
that provide for fast and easy
placement.
Meets your needs for a
variety of indications.
The 3M F2000 compomer
restorative system is indicated
for restoring a number of cavity
classifications in adult and
primary teeth. Indications
include:
• Class III and V cavities
• Class I and II cavities
in primary teeth
• Cervical erosion/abrasion
lesions
• Temporary repair of
fractured teeth
• Laminate or open sandwich
technique
• Core buildup where at least half
of the coronal tooth structure is
remaining to provide support
for the crown
Direct Compomer Restorative Technique
F2000 compomer restorative with 3M Single Bond adhesive system used in a Class V anterior restoration.
Please refer to instructions for precautionary, detailed use, storage and warranty information.
• Apply 3M™ Scotchbond™
etchant to enamel and
dentin for 15 seconds.
• Rinse and blot dry.
• Apply two consecutive coats
of 3M Single Bond adhesive
to enamel and dentin.
• Gently dry 2-5 seconds.
• Light cure the adhesive
for 10 seconds.
• Place F2000 compomer
restorative in increments.
• Light cure each increment
for 40 seconds.
• Use the 3M™ Sof-Lex™
Finishing and Polishing
System and 3M™ Sof-Lex™
Strips for finishing and
polishing the restoration.
